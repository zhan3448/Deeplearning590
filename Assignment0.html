Q: 2.1 Explain in your own words the bias-variance tradeoff

The bias-variance tradeoff states that there is a tradeoff between a model's ability to minimize the bias and variance. In general, a flexible model tends to have a higher variance and lower bias, and vice versa.

Q: 2.2 What role does regularization play in this tradeoff?

Regulation is the technique to avoid overfitting, which the model is way too flexible with a high variance but low bias. Regulation technique makes a small modification to introduce the bias into the bias that can reduce the variace. In that case, the regulation technigue can generalize better model.

Q: 2.3 Make reference to your findings in number (1) to describe models of high/low bias and variance

Given the excersie from the problem set number(1), we find that the lambda impacts the penalty. In other words, regulaztion penalizes the coefficients by conrolling the lambda. In our case, we found that the smaller lambda yields the higher coefficients, therefore, it leads to a lower bias.
